ABOUT ME

Hi. I'm Omojosola Juwon Ezekiel, a Geology graduate with a strong passion for groundwater recharge, contamination, and sustainable water resource management. My journey into groundwater research began in my sophomore year during an internship with the Oyo State Rural Water Supply and Sanitation Agency. That experience ignited a deep interest in groundwater exploration — from data collection and interpretation to borehole rehabilitation.
Recognizing the importance of geospatial tools in water research, I pursued training at the National Space Research and Development Agency, where I worked closely with experts in Geographical Information Systems (GIS). Since then, I’ve built hands-on experience using ArcGIS, Eokis Imaging, and other remote sensing tools to analyze groundwater flow and model erosion patterns.
Today, my research interests focus on: Groundwater recharge and contamination Geospatial analysis of groundwater systems Integrating machine learning and deep learning into groundwater depletion models and conservation strategies
I'm passionate about leveraging technology to develop smarter, more sustainable solutions for groundwater management.
